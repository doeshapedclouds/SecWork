## Executive Summary

**Status:** ACTIVE INVESTIGATION | **Severity:** HIGH | **DFU Restore Performed:** 2026-07-04

A suspected persistence mechanism has been identified across TWO Apple devices:

| Device | Model | Chip | OS Version |
|--------|-------|------|------------|
| Device 1 | MacBook Neo | A18 Pro | macOS 26.5.2 (Build 25F84) |
| Device 2 | Mac (secondary) | M4 | macOS Tahoe |

Despite a DFU restore performed on July 4th, 2026, multiple anomalous indicators suggest state preservation across system resets. **Critically, identical legacy bundle artifacts appear on BOTH devices**, including iPhone 5c identifiers and macOS Catalina-era descriptors. This cross-device, cross-architecture replication indicates entitlement injection rather than architecture-specific artifacts.

The attack architecture appears to leverage Real-Time Bidding (RTB) advertising infrastructure as a delivery vector, with evidence pointing toward manipulation of accessibility subsystems, MobileAsset daemon disruption, and APFS snapshot-based state preservation.

### Critical Hardware Note

The A18 Pro chip is derived from iPhone 16 Pro architecture. However, the iPhone 5c bundle pollution is NOT an architecture-specific artifact — it appears identically on the separate M4 Mac running macOS Tahoe. This confirms the phenomenon is a systemic injection mechanism operating at the LaunchServices/entitlement layer, not tied to specific OS install, silicon family, or device architecture.
